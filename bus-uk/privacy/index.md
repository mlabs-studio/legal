# Bus UK — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 28 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how **Xuan Toan Mai** trading as mlabs studio ("we", "us", "our") handles information in connection with the **Bus UK** mobile application ("the App").

## Summary

- We do **not** require an account.
- We do **not** sell your data.
- We do **not** show ads or share data with advertisers.
- We use your device location only while you are using the App, to find nearby bus stops.
- We collect usage analytics that do not directly identify you, and you can turn these off in the App's Settings at any time.

## 1. Information we handle

### 1.1 Location data

The App requests permission to access your device's location **while the App is in use**. Your approximate location is sent to Transport for London ("TfL") to find nearby bus stops and arrival predictions. We do not store your location on our servers.

To open the map near you on future launches, the App may store your last known location **locally on your device** (in the App's own storage). This local copy is not sent to our servers. It is replaced when the App saves a newer location and is removed when you delete the App.

Location data is transmitted only when required to perform nearby-stop or geocoding searches and is **not used for tracking your movements over time**.

You can deny or revoke location permission at any time in iOS Settings. Without it, the App cannot automatically find stops near you, but you can still search manually.

### 1.2 Usage analytics

We use [PostHog](https://posthog.com/) to collect usage analytics that **do not directly identify you**, such as:

- App usage events (for example: opening the App, viewing arrivals, viewing a route, switching map mode).
- General device information (model, iOS version, app version, locale).
- A pseudonymous identifier generated by PostHog. Under UK GDPR a persistent device identifier is treated as personal data, so we describe the data as pseudonymous rather than fully anonymous.

We do **not** collect your name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, or other directly identifying information. PostHog is configured with the "Discard client IPs" project setting enabled, which causes PostHog's ingest servers to drop your IP address upon receipt without persisting it. No IP is stored, so no location can be derived from one.

PostHog's own privacy policy is available at: https://posthog.com/privacy

**Consent**: on first launch, the App asks whether you want to share this analytics and crash data. If you choose "Not now", nothing is sent. You can change your choice at any time via Settings → Privacy → "Anonymous usage analytics".

### 1.3 Crash and error reports

The App may log technical errors to help us diagnose problems. These reports may contain technical diagnostic information such as device type, operating system version, app version, and error details (including stack traces). We do not intentionally collect information such as your name, email address, contacts, or photos in these reports, and we filter known location-shaped data (such as UK postcodes and coordinates) out of error messages before they are sent.

We use two services to receive these reports:

- [PostHog](https://posthog.com/) — for errors caught within the App's JavaScript layer.
- [Sentry](https://sentry.io/) — additionally for native (iOS) crashes and unhandled errors that PostHog cannot see.

Crash and error reports are governed by the same consent as analytics above.

### 1.4 Feedback emails

If you choose to send feedback via the "Send Feedback" link in Settings, your email app may send us your email address and any message you include. Error-context feedback may include app version, build number, device platform, OS version, the bus stop name and ID you were viewing, and a short technical error description. You can edit or remove any of this information before sending.

## 2. Third parties

We share data with the following services for app functionality, postcode and place search, analytics, and error diagnosis:

| Service | Purpose | Data shared |
|---|---|---|
| Transport for London (TfL) Unified API | Bus stops, arrivals, route data | Your approximate location, only while you use the App |
| Postcodes.io | UK postcode autocomplete in the search box | Partial postcode text you type |
| Apple geocoding services | Converting searched places and postcodes into map coordinates | Search text submitted for geocoding |
| PostHog (EU region) | Product analytics, JavaScript-layer error reports | Pseudonymous device ID, event names, device metadata |
| Sentry (EU region) | Native crash and unhandled-error reports | Stack traces, device metadata, app version |

Analytics and error-report data are processed by PostHog and Sentry on our behalf. We have selected the **EU hosting region** for both, so this data is processed in the European Economic Area.

When the App makes a request to any of the above services, your device's IP address is necessarily disclosed to that service as part of the network connection. PostHog discards it on receipt (see above); the other services receive it at the network layer and handle it under their own privacy policies.

We do not share data with advertisers, data brokers, or any other third party.

## 3. Legal basis (UK / EU users)

Under the UK GDPR and EU GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases:

- **Consent** — for access to your device location (granted via the iOS system prompt and revocable at any time in iOS Settings).
- **Consent** — for usage analytics and crash reporting (granted via the first-launch in-app prompt; revocable at any time via the toggle in Settings).
- **Legitimate interests** — for handling location data in transit to TfL solely to fulfil your request for nearby-stop information.

## 4. Your rights

If you are in the UK or EU, you have rights under data protection law including the right to access, correct, or delete personal data, to object to processing, to restrict processing, and to data portability.

Because the App does not collect data that identifies you personally and does not require an account, we typically have no personal data to retrieve or delete. If you believe we hold personal data about you, contact us at the email below.

To exercise your privacy rights or ask questions about this policy, contact us at the email in section 9.

## 5. Children's privacy

The App is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages, but is not directed specifically at children. We do not knowingly collect directly identifying information from children. If analytics is enabled, the App may collect the pseudonymous analytics and diagnostic data described in section 1.

## 6. Data retention

- Location data: not stored on our servers. The App may store your last known location locally on your device; it is replaced when a newer location is saved and removed when you delete the App.
- Analytics events: retained by PostHog for 12 months and then deleted. See PostHog's [data-retention policy](https://posthog.com/privacy) for details.
- Crash and error reports: retained by Sentry for 12 months and then deleted. See Sentry's [data-retention policy](https://sentry.io/legal/privacy/) for details.

## 7. Security

We use HTTPS for all network requests. No transmission method is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.
